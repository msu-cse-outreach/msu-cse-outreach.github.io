Week 2

Let's get started!

We made it through our first week! We'd like to thank and congratulate our students for their energy and enthusiasm; our students' parents for their ongoing support and encouragement; our mentors for making each Tuesday and Saturday exciting, engaging, and entertaining; and our advisors for their organization, coordination and commitment to making this a reality. Without you all, we wouldn't be here.

Computer Science is challenging and exciting, learning to code is so important to teach students how to communicate and think logically. Coding helps students understand the world around them better. Coding fosters creativity and allows students to apply their creative thinking skills. Joining the MSU x Belize Pathlight Program is a great way to learn these skills in a challenging, engaging, and positive environment. The challenges you overcome, the views you enjoy, and the friends you make are all apart about enjoying the journey and what it's all about.

Before we dive into a full recap of last weeks meetings, we've got a few announcements.

Meeting Recap

In preparation for next Saturday's final project fair, we kicked off last Saturday's meeting with a "Project Show and Tell" icebreaker. Students shared rough drafts of their final projects, exchanged ideas, traded compliments, drew inspiration from one another, and had a blast showing off their hard work!

We'd like to keep our students' creations a surprise for the final project fair, so we'll hold off on including any previews in this week's newsletter. Of course, you're always welcome to ask your own student to share what they've been working on! What did they have to say in response to this week's discussion questions?

  • Did you decide to do a spin off or a new project?
  • What do you want to try to add on today?
  • What part was the easiest so far?
  • Is there a part that's been tricky to figure out?
  • What is your favorite part of your project so far?

Following show-and-tell, we dove into an interactive spotlight highlighting the broad intersection between art, games and computer science. Computer science is often perceived as meticulous and monotonous—yet in reality, this stereotype is far from the truth. In fact, code empowers creativity, a colorful imagination, and an anything-is-possible spirit!

Students saw this first hand on Saturday by exploring Google's AutoDraw and Puzzle Party, an artistic Fluid Simulation engine, and the open-ended, create-your-own-world Sandspiel environment. Ask them to show you their favorite site—and while you're at it, ask why they think creativity is important in computer science!


Simulations and games highlighting the intersection of code and creativity from this week's spotlight. From left to right: AutoDraw, Puzzle Party, a Fluid Simulation engine, and Sandspiel.

After our spotlight, students continued working on their final projects, adding finishing touches and getting their code in tip-top shape for next Saturday's final project fair. We can't wait to see you there—we promise you'll be impressed! Our students have done amazing work over the last 9 weeks.

Speaking of the final project fair: our Gather space is decked out and looking good! Hannah, one of our lead mentors, did an amazing job decorating and setting up "interactive" posters which display students' final projects. During the final project fair, students, parents, and siblings will be able to explore this space, walking between posters and viewing a variety of final projects.


Our virtual venue for the final project fair! Shoutout to Hannah for the amazing decorations.

We ended Saturday's meeting with a discussion looking back on how far we've come in the course of 9 weeks. Time flies when you're learning, growing, and having fun!

As always, let us know if there's anything we can do to improve MSU x Belize —send us a note at belizecc@egr.msu.edu !

Spring 2022

We are excited to launch the first SS22 MSU x Pathlight Coding Club on Saturday, January 15! Thanks to the generocity of our sponsors and supporters, we were able to approve 60 students to enroll this semester. Participants will be grouped into 3 cohorts of about 20 each for remote club meetings by Zoom. Each cohort will follow the same curriculum, but proceed at their own pace in cohort-specific (synchronous) Zoom meetings.

To attend club meetings by Zoom, a participant must have:

  • Submitted a MSU x Pathlight Coding Club Confirmation form.
  • Returned a signed Parent Consent form (.pdf).
Please notify belizecc@egr.msu.edu if you experience difficulty with either form.

An email will be sent to participants notifying them of the Zoom information for their cohort by the end of day Tuesday, January 11. Please white-list belizecc@egr.msu.edu so that your mailer does not label mail that we send you as spam. Send an email to belizecc@egr.msu.edu if you have not received a notice with your cohort information by Wednesday morning.
