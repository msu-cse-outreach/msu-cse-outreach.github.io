Week 5

Fun with Functions

We're surrounded by black boxes—tools that just work when used to complete a task. To most of us, cars are black boxes getting us from point A to point B; TVs are black boxes streaming video and audio into our living room; and phones are black boxes connecting us to our friends and family through social media, texts and calls. Black boxes make our world better—they make complex tasks easy and empower us to accomplish more with less time and energy. It's impossible to imagine modern life without them!

In computer science, these black boxes take the form of functions—structures that hide the details of a complex task behind a simple statement. Functions just work, allowing us to think about other, higher-level concepts when writing code. This week, our coders learned how to write and call their own functions in Python—though you might just say they had fun with functions!

Meeting Recap

Students kicked off this week by getting to know each other and their mentors with a name-sharing icebreaker. Students got the chance to introduce themselves to some of the mentors and classmates while simultaneously trying to remember everyone else's name in their group.

After the icebreaker, Saturday's lesson began with an uplifting spotlight video from Khan Academy. Introducing the definition of a growth mindset and how it differs from a fixed mindset, the spotlight underscored the importance of bringing a growth mindset to Technovation every week. Learning to code is difficult, but the challenges we overcome as we navigate this learning process will ultimately make us stronger—we simply need to change the way we see failure! If we view it as an opportunity for improvement instead of a setback, we'll go far—if we're feeling down, we simply need to turn our frown upside-down and remember that all experiences are learning experiences!


Bringing a growth mindset to Technovation each week is key! Ask your student what a growth mindset means to them, and how they can apply it elsewhere in their life.

Next, we jumped into our coding lesson for the week.


Once students learned how to tell Tracy to change colors, they were able to change the color of the trail Tracy leaves.

After students learned how to change the color of Tracy's pen with the color command, students learned how to fill in the shapes they had drawn with the begin_fill and end_fill commands. Students also learned how to increase the thickness of trail Tracy leaves with the pensize command.


Students learned how to tell Tracy to increase the pensize and fill in shapes, they were able to keep track of the thickness of the shapes and trails they made!

Finally, we learned how to use the setposition and speed commands to fine-tune Tracy's behavior: setposition allows Tracy to move to specific positions on the screen, while speed allows Tracy to move faster or slower! Ask your student to show you these commands in action—they're pretty cool!


With the setposition command, Tracy's only a hop, skip, and a jump away from anywhere on the screen... and with the speed command, she can get there in a flash!
